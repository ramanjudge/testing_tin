The Problem We're Solving

India Has No Shortage
of Vendors. Only of
Integration.

TiNAAK is built on a single observation — one that recurred across 26 years of enterprise operations in India. Organisations don't fail because they can't find vendors. They fail because no single vendor takes full ownership across functions.

01
The Compliance Risk

Most organisations discover their statutory exposure at audit — not before. By then, the liability is already theirs. India's four Labour Codes, PF, ESIC and contract labour regulations are not optional — they are the baseline every operation must be built on from day one.

02
The Fragmentation Problem

A typical 200-person company manages 6–10 separate service vendors — each with their own contract, escalation path, and accountability gap. Coordinating them costs more than most organisations realise.

03
The Expertise Deficit

FM and HR knowledge in India's growing mid-market is overwhelmingly informal — processes improvised, KPIs unmeasured, vendor contracts unstructured. TiNAAK brings 26 years of enterprise-grade practice to organisations that can't yet afford to build it internally.
